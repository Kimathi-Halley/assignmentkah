#4
Evaluate:  $$\lim_{x\to -1} \Bigl[(x^4-3x)(x^2+5x+3)\Bigr]$$

Step 1: both factors are polynomials, and polynomials are continuous. By the Direct Substitution Property, we can substitute \(x=-1\) directly.

Step 2: Evaluate each factor at \(x=-1\):
- For $x^4-3x$: $(-1)^4-3(-1)=1+3=4.$ (Power Law, Sum/Difference Law)
- For $x^2+5x+3$: $(-1)^2+5(-1)+3=1-5+3=-1.$ (Power Law, Sum/Difference Law)

Step 3: By the Product Law, multiply the limits:
$$\lim_{x\to -1} \Bigl[(x^4-3x)(x^2+5x+3)\Bigr] = 4 \times (-1) = -4.$$

Answer: $-4$

Consider $f(x)=\bigl(x+2x^3\bigr)^4$ and $a=-1$. We want to show $f$ is continuous at $x=-1$, i.e., $$\lim_{x\to -1}f(x)=f(-1).$$

Step 1: Evaluate $f(-1)$ directly.
$$ f(-1)=\bigl((-1)+2(-1)^3\bigr)^4=\bigl(-1+2(-1)\bigr)^4=\bigl(-1-2\bigr)^4=(-3)^4=81. $$

Step 2: Compute the limit as $x\to -1$.
Because $x+2x^3$ is a polynomial (hence continuous), and the function $u^4$ is also continuous, we can apply the Limit Laws and substitute directly: $$ \lim_{x\to -1}\bigl(x+2x^3\bigr)^4 =\Bigl(\lim_{x\to -1}\bigl(x+2x^3\bigr)\Bigr)^4 =\bigl(-1+2(-1)\bigr)^4 =(-3)^4=81. $$

Step 3: Conclude continuity at $x=-1$.
Since $$ \lim_{x\to -1} f(x)=81=f(-1), $$ $f$ is continuous at $x=-1$ by the definition of continuity.
