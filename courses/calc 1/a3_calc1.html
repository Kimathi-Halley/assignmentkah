#4
Evaluate:  $$\lim_{x\to -1} \Bigl[(x^4-3x)(x^2+5x+3)\Bigr]$$

Step 1: both factors are polynomials, and polynomials are continuous. By the Direct Substitution Property, we can substitute \(x=-1\) directly.

Step 2: Evaluate each factor at \(x=-1\):
- For \(x^4-3x\):  \( (-1)^4-3(-1)=1+3=4.\)  (Power Law, Sum/Difference Law)
- For \(x^2+5x+3\):  \( (-1)^2+5(-1)+3=1-5+3=-1.\)  (Power Law, Sum/Difference Law)

Step 3: By the Product Law, multiply the limits:
$$\lim_{x\to -1} \Bigl[(x^4-3x)(x^2+5x+3)\Bigr] = 4 \times (-1) = -4.$$

Final Answer: $$-4$$

 $t^2 - 9 = (t - 3)(t + 3)$

 $\displaystyle \frac{t^2 - 9}{2t^2 + 7t + 3}

Evaluate:   $$\lim_{x\to 16} \frac{4-\sqrt{x}}{16x-x^2}.$$

numerator $$4-\sqrt{x}=\frac{(4-\sqrt{x})(4+\sqrt{x})}{4+\sqrt{x}} =\frac{16-x}{4+\sqrt{x}}.$$

denominator factor: $$16x-x^2=x(16-x).$$

$$\lim_{x\to 16} \frac{16-x}{(4+\sqrt{x})\,x(16-x)} =\lim_{x\to 16} \frac{1}{x(4+\sqrt{x})}.$$

Substitute \(x=16\): $$\frac{1}{16(4+\sqrt{16})}=\frac{1}{16(4+4)} =\frac{1}{16\cdot8}=\frac{1}{128}.$$

Answer: \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{128}\).

#32
Evaluate   $$\lim_{h\to0}\frac{\frac{1}{(x+h)^2}-\frac{1}{x^2}}{h}.$$

numerator: $$\frac{1}{(x+h)^2}-\frac{1}{x^2}=\frac{x^2-(x+h)^2}{x^2(x+h)^2}.$$

simplify: $$(x+h)^2=x^2+2xh+h^2 \quad\Rightarrow\quad x^2-(x^2+2xh+h^2)=-2xh-h^2.$$

$$\frac{x^2-(x+h)^2}{x^2(x+h)^2}=\frac{-2xh-h^2}{x^2(x+h)^2}.$$

$$\frac{-2xh-h^2}{h\cdot x^2(x+h)^2}=\frac{-2x-h}{x^2(x+h)^2}.$$

Take the limit as \(h\to0\): $$\lim_{h\to0}\frac{-2x-h}{x^2(x+h)^2}=\frac{-2x}{x^2\cdot x^2}=\frac{-2x}{x^4}=-\frac{2}{x^3}.$$

Answer: \(\displaystyle -\frac{2}{x^3}\).

Given   $$g(x)=\frac{x^2+x-6}{|x-2|}.$$

$$x^2+x-6=(x+3)(x-2).$$

For \(x>2\): \(|x-2|=x-2\), so $$g(x)=\frac{(x+3)(x-2)}{x-2}=x+3.$$
For \(x<2\): \(|x-2|=-(x-2)\), so $$g(x)=\frac{(x+3)(x-2)}{-(x-2)}=-(x+3).$$

(i) As \(x\to2^+\): \(g(x)=x+3\), so $$\lim_{x\to2^+}g(x)=2+3=5.$$
(ii) As \(x\to2^-\): \(g(x)=-(x+3)\), so $$\lim_{x\to2^-}g(x)=-(2+3)=-5.$$

Since the left- and right-hand limits are different, the limit as \(x\to2\) does not exist.
